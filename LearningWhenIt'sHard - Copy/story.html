Vorath’s Maw

Long ago, the Skarnex were a scattered race of scavengers, feeding on asteroid husks and derelict stations. That changed when Vorath, a rogue bio-engineer exiled from the Velarian Concord, crash-landed on a Skarnex moon. Obsessed with merging organic life and machine, Vorath used forbidden tech to uplift the Skarnex into a hive-mind species—his final experiment before being consumed by his own creation. Vorath’s Maw was built from the remains of his lab, fused with his DNA and consciousness. The ship is not just a vessel—it’s a living monument to their evolution and their first god.

2. The Maw Awakens
The ship lay dormant for centuries, buried beneath the crust of the moon Khar’Zuun. When the Skarnex hive reached critical mass, the planet cracked open and Vorath’s Maw emerged, fully sentient and hungry. It didn’t just fly—it hunted. The ship’s mandibles tore through orbital defenses, and its claws dragged satellites into its belly for assimilation.

3. The Hive’s Beacon
Vorath’s Maw now serves as the central nervous system of the Skarnex swarm. It emits a psychic pulse that keeps the hive synchronized across light-years. Wherever it travels, lesser Skarnex ships follow like insects to pheromones. Entire civilizations have gone mad from exposure to its signal, hearing whispers of Vorath’s voice in their dreams.

4. The Velarian Prophecy
The Velarians—those sleek, serene architects of the stars—have long feared the return of Vorath’s creation. Their ancient texts speak of a “Maw of Memory” that will consume the stars unless “the tide-born flame” rises. Some believe this refers to a Velarian pilot destined to infiltrate and destroy the ship from within. Others think the prophecy is a trap laid by Vorath himself.

Nytherion

Long before the Velarians mastered telepathy, there was only the Void—a realm of pure thought and emotion. Nytherion was not built, but summoned from that silence by the first Velarian empath, Serai Veyna, during a celestial convergence known as the Veilfall. It emerged fully formed, its hull etched with patterns no tool could replicate, its systems responding to emotion rather than code

The Resolute

It wasn’t designed to impress. It was designed to end dependency. With modular cargo bays, self-repairing hull segments, and a propulsion system that recycles gravitational stress, The Resolute can haul entire ecosystems across star systems with minimal fuel consumption.

Its AI core, VEX-9, doesn’t speak in metaphors. It speaks in metrics. And it never sleeps.

The ship’s maiden voyage was a test: a 12-planet supply run across the volatile Tarn Rift, where solar storms shred lesser vessels. The Resolute returned not only intact—but ahead of schedule, with surplus energy and zero crew casualties.

Since then, it’s become a symbol—not of Karnari pride, but of their philosophy: Efficiency is elegance. Precision is power.

The Sentinel

Kalen Vire was born in the twilight of Earth’s ambition. His world had long abandoned space travel, deeming it a relic of a reckless past. The skies were quiet, the launchpads rusted, and the stars were just distant lights—romantic, unreachable.

But Kalen was different.

From childhood, he devoured ancient star charts, built crude telescopes from salvaged glass, and scribbled equations on the backs of ration slips. His obsession wasn’t fame or conquest—it was connection. He believed the universe was alive, waiting to be known.
Yet the world mocked him. “There’s nothing out there,” they said. “We have enough problems down here.”

So he built in secret.

In a forgotten cavern beneath the ruins of an old observatory, Kalen spent decades crafting a vessel—not from metal, but from bio-synthetic tissue, quantum-threaded bone, and neural plasma. He wasn’t building a rocket. He was building a body. One that could survive the void, think faster than light, and carry his consciousness across galaxies.

He called it The Sentinel—not as a weapon, but as a witness. A man reborn as a living ship, forged not by a government, but by longing.

When he finally launched, there was no crowd. No countdown. Just silence… and then a streak of light across the sky.

Kalen didn’t leave Earth to escape it. He left to remind it what it had forgotten: That the stars are not beyond us. They are within us.
